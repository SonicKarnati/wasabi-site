AI learning and Empowerment Initiative - Concept Note 
 
A. Context & Opportunity 
Direct benefit transfers and financial support schemes, while essential, stop short of enabling 
long-term self-sufficiency. True welfare lies in building an individual's capacity to identify 
problems, design solutions, and believe they can lead change. This document presents a proof-
of-concept that places AI as a tool of psychological and professional empowerment in the hands 
of SCBC communities. The lived experience of marginalized communities holds the deepest, 
most nuanced understanding of real problems. Our program is designed to unlock that 
knowledge — and translate it into action. 
B. Core Philosophy 
Empowerment begins when people believe: “I am capable. I can build. I can solve. For myself 
and others.” Once individuals experience this shift, welfare transforms from support delivery 
into capability creation. 
Beyond Literacy This is not an AI teaching program. It is a program of 
psychological empowerment. When individuals from 
marginalised communities build something real — in their own 
language, with their own context — they start believing they 
can. That belief is the most powerful form of welfare 
In Their Language Every session is delivered in the language of the community. 
Every example, every use case, every outcome is rooted in their 
lived context — not an abstract or corporate framing. 
Learn, Then Build Theory without application fades. Every participant goes 
through a learning phase followed by a live building session, 
producing a real first product, solution, or workflow — in the 
room, on the day. 
 
C. Three Target Groups 
1. School Students (Class 9–12, SCBC) 
Aspirational students at a critical juncture, ready to shape their futures if given the right tools 
• Use AI to learn faster, understand concepts better, and score higher  
• Discover scholarships, career opportunities, and competitive exam resources using AI  
• Build income potential through freelancing and gig work facilitated by AI tools  
• Develop a portfolio project they can present with pride 
 
2. Women SHG Leaders (SCBC Women-Led SHGs)

• Use AI as a co-founder on their phones — accessible, voice-first, no English required  
• Overcome barriers in marketing, customer communication, and sales using AI chatbots  
• Explore new income streams and digital market access  
• Target cohort: 20–30 women SHG leaders  
 
3. Persons with Physical Disability 
Individuals with disability often face compounded barriers to opportunity. AI can level that field. 
• Use AI tools suited to their specific accessibility needs  
• Build vocational or entrepreneurial skills using available infrastructure  
• Demonstrate that disability is not a limit to building or leading  
• Conducted at or near an existing vocational centre with basic digital access 
 
D. Program Format 
Duration 6 hours 
Learning Phase ~2.5 hrs: AI fundamentals, tools, prompting, 
use cases — fully contextualized 
Building Phase ~2.5 hrs: Participants build a real first 
solution, live, with hand-holding support 
Closing ~1 hr: Demonstration and certification for all 
participants 
Language Delivered in the local language of 
participants; examples from their context 
Device Mobile first approach.  
Prerequisite Basic digital familiarity (has used a phone or 
computer); no coding required

F. Outcomes 
1. Community-Led Problem Identification: Once individuals have the tools and confidence to 
build, they begin articulating problems that no external researcher or policy team has ever 
surfaced. The most accurate, actionable problem statements for SCBC welfare will come from 
SCBC communities themselves — this program creates the conditions for that to happen. 
2. A Shift in Self-Belief: Participants leave not just with skills, but with a fundamentally different 
perception of themselves. The experience of building something real — in their own language, 
on their own terms — triggers a psychological shift that no certificate or cash transfer can 
replicate. This confidence ripples outward into family, community, and peer networks. 
3. An Accelerator Across Every Life Domain: AI is not a single skill — it is a force multiplier. A 
student uses it to study smarter and crack competitive exams. A woman entrepreneur uses it to 
market her business. A person with disability uses it to access opportunities previously out of 
reach. The same capability accelerates learning, skilling, employment, and enterprise — 
simultaneously. 
4. A Demonstrable Portfolio, Not Just Attendance: Every participant leaves with something they 
built. Not a certificate of participation, but a tangible proof of creation — a solution, a tool, a 
workflow. This becomes the first entry in a professional portfolio that can open doors in ways a 
marksheet alone cannot. 
5. A Replicable Model for the Department: This proof-of-concept is designed to be 
documented, measured, and scaled. The department gains a tested, ground-level playbook for 
AI-led empowerment that can be replicated across districts, target groups, and welfare 
schemes — without reinventing the wheel each time. 
G. Why This Matters 
Financial support empowers people for a season. The belief that "I can build, I can solve, I can 
lead for myself and others" empowers them for a lifetime. The most profound shift in welfare 
comes not from the outside in, but from the inside out. When an SCBC student builds their first 
AI-powered solution in a room full of peers — when a woman SHG leader sends her first AI-
drafted marketing message in her own language — something changes permanently. That is the 
outcome we are here to create.
