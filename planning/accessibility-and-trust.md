# Accessibility and Trust Checklist

## Why This Doc Exists

This document defines the non-negotiable accessibility and credibility rules for the site. It exists so implementation can move fast without drifting into unreadable, inaccessible, or overclaimed UI.

## Accessibility Rules

### Structure

- One H1 per page.
- Use logical heading order.
- Use semantic landmarks for header, nav, main, footer, sections, and forms.

### Contrast

- Keep text readable on cream and forest surfaces.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate proof status.
- Button hover states must remain legible against surrounding text.

### Keyboard Support

- All interactive elements must be reachable and usable by keyboard.
- Mobile navigation must work with keyboard and Escape.
- Focus states must be visible and not subtle.

### Reduced Motion

- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Do not make meaning depend on animation.

### Forms

- Every field needs a visible label.
- Required state and errors must be explicit.
- Do not imply that a form submission creates a partnership or enrollment.

### Mobile Usability

- Keep touch targets reasonable.
- Prevent overflow in buttons, cards, and nav.
- Keep layouts simple before adding polish.

## Trust Rules

### Claim Safety

- No unsupported impact claims.
- No fake partner logos.
- No fake testimonials.
- No public-sector naming without approval.
- No third-party quotes without permission and context.

### Proof Handling

- Separate verified, projected, placeholder, approved, and needs-verification states.
- Every public metric needs a source trail.
- Stories require consent and rights clearance.

### Contact Transparency

- Use one official contact path once confirmed.
- Keep contact details explicit if they are still placeholders.
- Make inquiry type visible.

### Visual Trust

- Use restraint, whitespace, and calm typography.
- Avoid sci-fi AI visuals and busy promotional treatment.
- Prefer simple evidence presentation over decorative persuasion.

## Acceptance Check

- The page is readable.
- The interaction is keyboard usable.
- The claim status is explicit.
- The visual hierarchy does not hide important trust information.
