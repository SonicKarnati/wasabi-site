Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`; `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`

Wasabi runs programs that help people experience AI through practical creation rather than abstract instruction. The source emphasizes a first real build, a shift in belief, and confidence as the beginning of capability.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`

### Who it serves

The materials imply students, teachers, people in villages, people in tier-2 cities, government school communities, and broader Bharat audiences who may feel excluded from technology.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`; `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Geographic or cultural context

The brand is explicitly Bharat-native. The source distinguishes Bharat from generic India language and emphasizes villages, tier-2 cities, government schools, real Bharat environments, and the future of Viksit Bharat.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`; `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Sector/category

AI literacy, practical AI creation, education/programming, human capability building, and public-interest technology. The source material does not define a formal legal category or business model.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_brand_voice.docx`

## 3. Visual Identity

### Logo files found

- `assets/raw/WhatsApp Image 2026-06-10 at 9.02.44 AM.jpeg`: dark-background Wasabi AI logo, white wordmark with green "AI".
- `assets/raw/WhatsApp Image 2026-06-10 at 9.02.45 AM.jpeg`: light-background Wasabi AI logo, dark wordmark with green "AI".

The design system also references the Wasabi mark as an interlocking circular symbol paired with a wordmark.

Source: `assets/raw/*`; `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Logo usage notes

- The mark and wordmark must appear together unless a specific alternate usage is approved.
- Use correct color versions for the background.
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the mark symbol.
- Minimum size: 120px wide digital; 30mm wide print.
- Do not stretch, distort, recolor outside approved versions, add effects, use wordmark alone, or use terracotta for the logo.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Color palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Deep Forest Dark | `#11241F` | Dark backgrounds, headings on light |
| Wasabi Green | `#2D6A4F` | Primary brand color, dominant |
| Soft Terracotta | `#E5784A` | Accent only, sparingly |
| Warm Cream | `#FFF4E3` | Base background, most surfaces |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Text on dark, clean space |

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Color rules

- Approved combinations include cream with dark text, cream with green text, green with white text, forest dark with white or cream text, terracotta with white for a single accent, and cream with terracotta for accent quotes or numbers.
- Avoid green and terracotta as text/background combinations.
- Avoid green text on forest dark for body copy.
- Terracotta is an accent, not a theme. Maximum one terracotta element per page or screen.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Typography

- Display/headlines: Cormorant Garamond.
- Body/interface: DM Sans.
- Display and H1/H2 should use Cormorant.
- H3 and smaller interface/body levels use DM Sans.
- Body copy should be left-aligned, readable, and generally no longer than 70 to 75 characters per line.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Graphic elements and illustration style

The visual language centers on a hand-drawn single line that connects people, ideas, and change.

Four defined styles:

- Real photo plus flowing line connection for hero moments and major visual statements.
- Real photo plus line outline/aura for spotlight stories and testimonials.
- Pure line illustration for diagrams, process flows, decks, reports, and app UI.
- Single concept illustration for philosophy pages and meditative brand moments.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Image style

- Use real students, real classrooms, real workshops, and real Wasabi environments wherever possible.
- Show people mid-action, building, presenting, or engaging with screens.
- Reflect real Bharat geography, not only urban or elite spaces.
- Represent boys and girls equally.
- Show genuine emotion, practical devices, and authentic environments.
- Desaturate images slightly when compositing with the palette.
- Use organic blob masking where possible.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Layout patterns

- Cream background throughout website surfaces.
- Alternate cream and forest dark sections; never two consecutive forest dark sections.
- Forest dark footer.
- Green primary buttons with white text.
- Green outline secondary buttons.
- Cards use cream background, thin green-mid border, and generous internal padding.
- Spacing scale: 8, 16, 24, 40, 80, 120px.
- Designs should breathe; crowded layouts signal insecurity.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`

### Design dos and don'ts

Do:

- Use fewer colors and more whitespace.
- Keep the human as the visual and conceptual center.
- Use Cormorant for gravitas and DM Sans for clarity.
- Use real Bharat imagery when available.
- Use terracotta only as a restrained accent.

Do not:

- Use gradients, text drop shadows, decorative borders, emoji as design elements, unsupported fonts, busy backgrounds behind body copy, generic global stock imagery, robotic/sci-fi AI imagery, justified text, overcrowded layouts, or terracotta as a dominant color.

Source: `docs/source/wasabi_design_system.docx`
