# Design Direction

## 1. Design Objective

The Wasabi website should visually communicate credibility, human capability, and grounded ambition. It should feel professional enough for government, institutional, and international visitors while still feeling warm, educational, and human for schools, parents, students, and community partners. The visual system should use the approved Wasabi identity with restraint, show program breadth without brochure clutter, and make proof status clear.

## 2. Visual Personality

- Credible.
- Modern.
- Calm.
- Structured.
- Human.
- Bharat-native.
- Future-facing without sci-fi styling.
- Institutional but not cold.
- Aspirational but not exaggerated.
- Premium but accessible.

## 3. Layout Principles

### Grid System

- Use a centered content container for most pages.
- Use a 12-column desktop grid for complex sections and 1-column mobile flow.
- Use 2-column layouts sparingly for explanation + proof, program comparison, or contact routing.
- Avoid dense brochure-style spreads on the website.

### Spacing

Use the design system spacing scale:

- XS: 8px
- S: 16px
- M: 24px
- L: 40px
- XL: 80px
- XXL: 120px

### Section Rhythm

- Alternate calm cream sections with occasional forest dark emphasis sections.
- Avoid two consecutive forest dark sections.
- Use one dominant idea per section.
- Use clear section headers, then cards/lists only when they clarify structure.

### Hero Structure

- Hero should establish Wasabi's identity and capability thesis quickly.
- Avoid landing-page spectacle.
- Use text, proof placeholders, and a single strong visual or line/photo treatment when approved assets exist.
- Do not use unsupported metrics in the hero.

### Whitespace

- Whitespace should signal confidence.
- Do not crowd hero, proof, or program sections.
- Long explanatory content should become tabs, accordions, comparison tables, or sectioned lists in later implementation.

### Content Density

- Government/institutional pages can carry more information, but should still scan cleanly.
- Program sections should show audience, format, outputs, proof status, and CTA.
- Impact sections should separate verified proof from planned/projected scale.

### Responsive Behavior

- Mobile should keep content linear and readable.
- Cards should stack vertically.
- Proof strips should become compact stat rows or stacked blocks.
- CTAs should remain visible and have large touch targets.

### Avoiding Clutter

- Do not show every program detail at once.
- Do not overload a section with metrics, partner logos, and testimonials simultaneously.
- Do not use campaign variants as the main site style.

## 4. Color Usage

### Primary Colors

- Deep Forest Dark: `#11241F`
- Wasabi Green: `#2D6A4F`
- Warm Cream: `#FFF4E3`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

### Accent Colors

- Soft Terracotta: `#E5784A`

### Neutral Colors

- Body text black from the design system: `#1A1A1A`
- Gray from the design system: `#6B6B6B`

### Background Colors

- Warm Cream should be the main site background.
- White can be used for digital utility surfaces and form inputs.
- Forest Dark should be used for footer, high-trust emphasis bands, quotes, or proof sections.
- Wasabi Green should be used sparingly for CTA backgrounds and compact callout blocks.

### Text Colors

- Body on cream/white: `#1A1A1A`.
- Headings on cream/white: Deep Forest Dark or Wasabi Green.
- Text on forest dark: White or Warm Cream.
- Text on Wasabi Green: White, with cream for secondary text if contrast is acceptable.

### Warning / Review Status Colors

No warning palette is specified in source materials. Use text labels first, such as `Needs verification`, `Projected`, and `Placeholder`. If visual warning tokens are needed later, they require human confirmation.

### Color Usage Rules

- Terracotta is accent-only, maximum one meaningful use per page/screen.
- Do not use terracotta as a large background.
- Do not place green and terracotta as competing equal elements.
- Do not use green text on forest dark body copy.
- Do not use gradients.
- Campaign palettes, such as Adda247 red/black or KASC event colors, should appear only as framed source/campaign evidence if approved.

## 5. Typography Direction

### Font Families

- Headlines/display: Cormorant Garamond.
- Body/interface: DM Sans.
- Fallbacks: Georgia for Cormorant Garamond; Arial or system sans-serif for DM Sans.

### Heading Style

- Major page statements should use Cormorant Garamond.
- Headings should be sentence case.
- Large headlines should not become paragraphs.
- Avoid negative letter spacing in implementation unless design review explicitly approves it.

### Body Text Style

- Body copy should use DM Sans.
- Keep body text left-aligned.
- Do not set body copy below 16px for web.
- Keep long-form line length around 70-75 characters.

### Institutional Readability Rules

- Use short paragraphs.
- Use labels for proof status.
- Avoid vague "AI revolution" language unless context is specific.
- Make placeholders visibly different from confirmed copy.

### Mobile Type Guidance

- Keep mobile headings strong but not oversized.
- Avoid viewport-scaled font sizes.
- Preserve readable line height.
- Ensure button text does not wrap awkwardly.

## 6. Imagery Direction

### Use

- Real students, classrooms, workshops, SHG/community settings, and Wasabi environments wherever rights are cleared.
- People mid-action: building, presenting, using phones or laptops, discussing outputs.
- Images that show real Bharat across geography and context.
- Organic masks and hand-drawn line treatments when supported by final assets.

### Avoid

- Generic global stock imagery.
- Robotic hands, holograms, circuit boards, sci-fi AI imagery.
- Photos where technology is the hero instead of people.
- Staged elite-only environments.
- Unapproved student or community photos.

### Best Fit by Page

- Home: one approved real human visual or restrained line/photo composition.
- About: human-context imagery and line illustration.
- Programs: program cards may use approved source thumbnails or icons.
- Impact: verified story images only.
- Contact: minimal imagery; prioritize clarity.

## 7. Graphic Language

### Shapes

- Use simple rectangular sections and modest cards.
- Organic blob masks may be used for approved photography.
- Avoid decorative blobs/orbs as backgrounds.

### Lines

- The hand-drawn single line is the signature motif.
- Use lines to connect concepts, people, or steps.
- Do not overuse decorative line dividers.

### Cards

- Use cards for repeated items: program cards, impact cards, story cards, inquiry paths.
- Keep card radius modest, 8px or less unless final design system changes it.
- Avoid cards inside cards.

### Icons

- Use simple, consistent line icons for program categories, inquiry types, and UI actions.
- Icons should clarify, not decorate.
- Avoid emoji as visual design elements.

### Data Visualization

- Use clear stat blocks, small comparison tables, and simple progress/timeline layouts.
- Always label metric status: verified, projected, target, placeholder, needs verification.

### Borders, Shadows, Texture, Glow

- Use thin borders in green/dark with low opacity.
- Avoid drop shadows on text.
- Avoid heavy shadows, glassmorphism, glow effects, and futuristic textures.

## 8. Government / Institutional Trust Rules

### Choices That Increase Trust

- Clear information hierarchy.
- Source-grounded wording.
- Conservative proof labels.
- Generous whitespace.
- Readable typography.
- Calm color usage.
- Explicit contact path.
- Program structure tables/cards.
- Accessibility-first forms.

### Choices That Weaken Trust

- Unsupported partner logos.
- Unverified large metrics.
- Generic AI visuals.
- Excessive animation.
- Overcrowded brochure sections.
- Startup hype language.
- Dark futuristic gradients.
- Fake testimonials or vague case studies.

## 9. What the Site Must Avoid

- Startup cliché.
- Over-animated AI website look.
- Fake futuristic gradients.
- Glassmorphism-heavy UI.
- Unsupported impact claims.
- Dense brochure dumping.
- Generic stock imagery.
- Weak or vague CTAs.
- Poor accessibility.
- Unapproved partner logos.
- Unverified testimonials.
- Campaign styles replacing the core Wasabi system.
