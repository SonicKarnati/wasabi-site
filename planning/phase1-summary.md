## Key Brand Findings

- Wasabi AI is positioned as a movement and capability-building organization, not a generic course company.
- The central belief is that AI expands what human beings are capable of becoming.
- The brand should focus on people becoming builders through practical creation.
- "The Wasabi Moment" is the named breakthrough when someone realizes they can create.
- The strongest repeated language centers on building, capability, confidence, ownership, creation, real Bharat, and human potential.
- Additional PDFs identify several program/campaign tracks: AI Learner, Adda247 AI Bootcamp, SHG Empowerment, Bharat Builds with Manus, KASC 2026, Startup with AI, and SCBC AI empowerment.
- The brand should avoid overused corporate and EdTech language, and public copy must avoid unsupported overclaims.

## Key Visual Findings

- The approved core palette is Deep Forest Dark `#11241F`, Wasabi Green `#2D6A4F`, Soft Terracotta `#E5784A`, Warm Cream `#FFF4E3`, and White `#FFFFFF`.
- Wasabi Green is the primary brand color. Terracotta is accent-only and should appear sparingly.
- Typography is defined as Cormorant Garamond for display/headlines and DM Sans for body/interface text.
- The visual signature is a hand-drawn single line used with real photos, outline/aura treatments, pure line illustration, or single concept illustrations.
- Photography should show real people, real classrooms, real workshops, real Bharat, and practical devices.
- The PDFs show campaign variants, including AI Learner lime/black youth identity, Adda247 red/black co-branding, KASC civic/event identity, and formal SHG/SCBC concept-note formats.

## Key Audience Findings

- Beneficiaries need to believe that AI is for them and that they can build something real.
- Government visitors likely need to see Wasabi as credible, practical, aligned with capability creation, and grounded in measured human outcomes.
- International visitors likely need Bharat-native context explained without flattening the brand into generic global AI language.
- Institutional partners likely need operational clarity, proof, program structure, impact data, and credibility signals.
- Schools, parents, SHG women, EFC leaders, SCBC communities, persons with physical disability, startup builders, and young India builders are supported by the expanded PDF materials.
- Donors, sponsors, volunteers, media, and ecosystem partners are implied in event materials but still need clearer role definitions.

## Major Missing Information

- Formal vision statement.
- Official primary public contact details and preferred domain.
- Legal entity details, if relevant.
- Current status for each named program, campaign, or initiative.
- Verified impact metrics and source records.
- Partner or institution proof and logo-use permissions.
- Award, certification, or media proof.
- Leadership/team bios and advisor publishing permissions.
- Verified testimonials and consent.
- Case-study permissions for named student/project examples.
- Rights-cleared photography from actual Wasabi environments.
- Production-ready vector or transparent logo files.
- Accessibility, consent, privacy, safeguarding, or student/youth policy guidance.
- Government-specific proof, approvals, and permitted public-sector naming.
- Donor, sponsor, volunteer, or institutional partnership flows.

## Risks or Assumptions

- The current materials provide strong brand direction and richer program evidence, but several credibility claims are not yet verified for public use.
- Public copy should be reviewed carefully to avoid drifting into unsupported claims.
- Government and institutional audiences are now better supported by SHG, SCBC, and KASC materials, but proof, permissions, and current program status still need human confirmation.
- The available logo files are JPEGs only. Production design will need better logo assets.
- The brand calls for real human photography, but no rights-cleared program photography was provided in Phase 1.
- Some PDF claims are high-risk for public use until verified, including third-party quotes/logos, partner claims, projected metrics, and student testimonials.

## Recommended Next Step for Phase 2

Proceed to Phase 2 site strategy and low-fidelity wireframes, using placeholders for unverified metrics, partner proof, testimonials, and public-sector claims. Before production website design begins, collect missing credibility and content inputs: measured impact, partner proof, official contact information, team bios, testimonials, case studies, and production logo/photo assets.
