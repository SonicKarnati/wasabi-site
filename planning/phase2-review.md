## 2. What Decisions Were Made

- Use a five-page version 1 site map: Home, About, Programs / Work, Impact, Contact.
- Keep all program details in one Programs / Work page for now, rather than creating separate public program pages.
- Treat AI Learner, SHG Empowerment, public-sector/SCBC POC, startup/hackathon programs, KASC/event programs, and co-branded bootcamps as program categories.
- Separate verified impact, planned/projected scale, and missing proof on the Impact page.
- Use placeholders for all unverified metrics, partner references, third-party quotes, public-sector claims, and student stories.
- Keep wireframes low-fidelity and structural only.

## 3. What Assumptions Were Made

- Phase 1 PR will be accepted or merged before Phase 2 is reviewed as the next checkpoint.
- The official Wasabi website should use the core Wasabi identity, not campaign-specific visual variants.
- Government and institutional visitors are primary trust audiences for version 1.
- Schools, parents, and students are important, but may not require separate microsites in version 1.
- Some program materials describe proposed or future initiatives, not completed proof.
- Contact details need human confirmation before production copy.

## 5. What Still Needs Human Review

- Official primary domain, email, phone number, and contact routing.
- Current status of each named program/campaign/initiative.
- Which programs should be public in version 1.
- Verified impact metrics and source records.
- Partner/logo permissions for Adda247, Manus, schools, government/public-sector references, and other institutions.
- Third-party quote permissions, especially OpenAI/Sam Altman references.
- Advisor/team names, roles, bios, and publishing permissions.
- Student story/testimonial/photo consent.
- Public-sector naming permissions for SCBC/Haryana/MoSJE-related material.
- Privacy, safeguarding, consent, and accessibility language.

## 6. What Must Be Resolved Before Phase 3

- Decide which metrics are approved for public website use.
- Decide official contact details and public domain.
- Confirm program statuses and version 1 program categories.
- Approve or remove partner/logo/third-party references.
- Approve at least one human story or decide to launch without stories.
- Provide production-ready logo assets and rights-cleared photography, or decide on placeholder/illustration strategy.
- Confirm whether the website will include enrollment CTAs, partnership CTAs, or both.

## 7. Recommended Next Phase

Phase 3 should create a content-first website blueprint: final page copy outline, source-supported CTA language, proof inventory, and low-fidelity content blocks ready for visual design. Phase 3 should still avoid production implementation until the verification questions above are answered.
