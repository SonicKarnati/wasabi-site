## 2. What Decisions Were Made

- The site should use the core Wasabi design system, not campaign-specific styles.
- The visual tone should be credible, modern, calm, structured, human, institutional, and future-facing without sci-fi styling.
- Terracotta remains accent-only.
- Campaign visual variants may appear only as approved evidence within program contexts.
- Components should include proof-status handling so unsupported claims do not slip into production.
- Motion should be subtle, optional, and accessibility-bound.
- Phase 4 should not begin full production until contact, content, proof, and asset decisions are confirmed.

## 3. What Assumptions Were Made

- The five-page structure from Phase 2 remains the version 1 site map.
- Government and institutional trust are the highest design priority.
- The core brand palette, typography, and spacing from the design system should guide the eventual build.
- Real photography is preferred when rights are cleared.
- Placeholders may remain in early implementation if clearly labeled.
- A future implementation may use Next.js, but Phase 3 does not implement it.

## 5. What Still Needs Human Review

- Visual direction and whether it should skew more institutional, student-friendly, or balanced.
- Component list and v1 priorities.
- Content model fields and proof-status workflow.
- Official design approval for provisional tokens such as radius, border opacity, and container width.
- Whether optional build-readiness recommendations match the desired Phase 4 direction.

## 6. What Must Be Resolved Before Phase 4

- Official domain, email, phone, and contact routing.
- Verified impact metrics and approved proof inventory.
- Current status of each program/campaign/initiative.
- Partner/logo permissions.
- Third-party quote permissions.
- Student/community story consent.
- Advisor/team bios and permissions.
- Production logo files and rights-cleared photography.
- Privacy, safeguarding, consent, and accessibility language.

## 7. Phase 4 Readiness

The project is ready for Phase 4 implementation planning, but not yet ready for a production website build. A safe Phase 4 can scaffold the site and build placeholder-safe static pages only after the mentor confirms content, proof, and visual direction priorities.
