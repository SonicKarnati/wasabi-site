# Wasabi AI Site Strategy

## Why This Doc Exists

This document explains the product logic behind the site. It tells developers what the website is trying to make a visitor believe, why the page set is limited, and why claim-safety is treated as a core feature instead of a content detail.

## Product Goal

The site should make Wasabi AI feel credible, practical, Bharat-native, and capable of turning AI exposure into real builder confidence. The website is not trying to be a broad marketing site. It is a trust-building product with conservative claims.

## Visitor Belief Model

The site has to support five core beliefs:

1. Wasabi is legitimate and serious.
2. Human capability is the product, not AI hype.
3. Wasabi understands Bharat contexts and learning environments.
4. Wasabi can run structured programs that produce real outputs.
5. It is safe to contact Wasabi for partnership, inquiry, or program discussion.

## Audience Groups

- Government and public-sector visitors need proof, structure, implementation clarity, and claim discipline.
- Institutional partners need program outlines, deliverables, and operating confidence.
- Schools and parents need beginner-friendly, safe, guided learning signals.
- Students and young builders need clarity that they can start without being experts.
- SHG and community stakeholders need practical, voice-first, regional-language framing.
- International visitors need context, not a generic AI education story.

## Why The Page Set Is Limited

Version 1 uses five pages because the source material supports broad categories, but not enough verified detail for many subpages.

- Home establishes trust and direction.
- About explains the philosophy.
- Programs shows the work without overpromising on specifics.
- Impact separates verified proof from planned scale.
- Contact converts interest into the right inquiry path.

## Content Rules

- Do not use unsupported claims, logos, testimonials, rankings, or awards.
- Keep verified, projected, placeholder, approved, and needs-verification states explicit.
- Keep proof separate from aspiration.
- Keep Bharat context specific and practical.
- Keep the language calm, institutional, and human.

## Visual Rules

- Use the cream, wasabi green, forest, terracotta, Cormorant Garamond, and DM Sans system.
- Avoid generic AI startup visuals, glassmorphism, sci-fi treatment, and flashy motion.
- Use visual restraint so trust reads first.

## Implementation Implications

- The homepage hero should establish identity and capability thesis quickly.
- Program cards should expose audience, format, outputs, and proof status.
- Impact cards should label measured vs projected vs placeholder content.
- Contact should present inquiry paths before asking for form input.
- Placeholders should be visible rather than hidden.

## What Not To Do

- Do not collapse strategy into marketing copy.
- Do not add extra pages just because the tree allows it.
- Do not introduce optimistic language that outruns the proof inventory.
- Do not treat phase review notes as the product source of truth.
